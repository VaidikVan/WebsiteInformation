Hummingbird

Overview

Hummingbirds are tiny colorful birds and are one of the most specialized nectar-eating animals. Hummingbirds are native to the Americas and are of the biological family Trochilidae.

Hummingbirds are very specialized creatures, and have many adaptions in order to enable maneuverable rapid flight. These adaptions include exceptional metabolic capacity, adaptations to high altitude, sensitive visual and communication abilities, and long-distance migration in some species. Among all types of birds, male hummingbirds have the widest diversity of colours.
